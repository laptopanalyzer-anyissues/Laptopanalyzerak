Frequently Asked Questions

Is Laptop Analyzer free?

Yes — completely free with no hidden costs, subscriptions, or premium tiers. Tests run directly in your browser.

Is my data safe?

Hardware tests run locally in your browser. We don't store your test results, and we've designed the tool to minimize unnecessary data handling.

Why isn't my camera or microphone working?

Your browser needs permission to access these devices. Click 'Allow' when prompted. If you previously denied access, check your browser's site settings.

Why isn't fn or F11 working on Mac?

The fn key is hardware-level and invisible to browsers. For F11, macOS intercepts it for Mission Control. Press fn + F11 together, or enable 'Use F1, F2, etc. as standard function keys' in System Preferences → Keyboard.

What should I do about dead pixels?

If under warranty, contact your manufacturer — dead pixels may be covered. Stuck pixels can sometimes be fixed with pixel-cycling tools, but dead pixels usually require a screen replacement.

Why is my speed test slow?

WiFi signal, distance from router, network congestion, and ISP throttling all affect results. Try a wired connection for the most accurate measurement.

Does this work on desktops?

Yes. While designed for laptops, all tests work on desktop computers. Some tests like touchpad won't apply, but display, keyboard, audio, and network work perfectly.

How accurate are the results?

Our tests use standard Web APIs and are reliable for detecting common hardware issues. For deeper diagnostics like SSD health or CPU thermals, dedicated software is needed.

Do I need to install anything?

No. Laptop Analyzer runs entirely in your browser — just open the site and start testing.

Complete Laptop Diagnostics & Hardware Health Guide

Keeping your laptop in top condition requires regular hardware diagnostics. Whether you are a student, professional, or gamer, understanding the health of your laptop's components helps you avoid unexpected failures and costly repairs. LaptopAnalyzer provides a comprehensive suite of browser-based diagnostic tests that cover every critical hardware component without requiring any software downloads or technical expertise.

Our dead pixel test cycles through solid color screens — red, green, blue, white, and black — allowing you to spot defective pixels on LCD, OLED, and IPS displays. Dead pixels appear as tiny dots that remain a single color regardless of what is displayed. Stuck pixels, on the other hand, may glow a persistent color and can sometimes be repaired using pixel-cycling techniques. Regular screen testing is especially important when purchasing used or refurbished laptops, as display defects are among the most common issues found in secondhand devices.

The keyboard tester detects every keypress in real time and maps it onto a virtual keyboard layout. This is invaluable for verifying mechanical keyboards, membrane keyboards, and laptop chiclet keyboards. You can quickly identify keys that fail to register, keys that double-type, or modifier keys that stick. Our tester supports ANSI, ISO, and JIS layouts, covering full-size, tenkeyless, and compact keyboards used worldwide.

Audio diagnostics include both microphone and speaker tests. The microphone test displays a real-time waveform as you speak, helping you confirm input levels and detect background noise or hardware distortion. The speaker test plays stereo audio through left and right channels independently, ensuring balanced output and identifying blown or malfunctioning drivers. These tests are essential before video conferences, online classes, or recording sessions.

Network speed testing measures your download speed, upload speed, and latency using Cloudflare's global infrastructure. Understanding your connection performance helps troubleshoot slow browsing, buffering videos, and laggy online gaming. We recommend testing on both WiFi and wired connections to isolate whether issues stem from your network adapter or your internet service provider. Combined with our webcam test, touchpad test, and USB port checker, LaptopAnalyzer gives you a complete picture of your laptop's hardware health — all from a single browser tab, completely free.
